Why SIP is required & benefits

Why SIP?

Systematic Investing removes timing risk, enforces discipline and leverages rupee-cost averaging — you buy more units when markets are low and fewer when high.

Benefits

  • Disciplined savings
  • Rupee-cost averaging
  • Power of compounding
  • Flexible amounts & step-ups

Why Step-up SIP?

Step-ups allow increasing contribution amounts annually (or periodically) to match salary growth — increasing corpus while keeping starting contributions manageable.

How Step-up SIP works with inflation

If your contributions remain flat but inflation erodes purchasing power, the real value of your corpus reduces. Step-up SIP increases contributions regularly — this helps maintain (or grow) real purchasing power. This calculator shows nominal value and inflation-adjusted value (real) using the inflation rate you provided.

How to use this calculator

  1. Enter the start year and the investment horizon (years).
  2. Set your initial monthly SIP and the expected annual step-up percentage (how much you'll increase SIP each year).
  3. Set your assumed annual return (nominal) and an inflation rate (we default to 4%).
  4. View the summary: total invested, nominal projected value, returns, and inflation-adjusted (real) value.
  5. Use the yearly projection table to see impact of each year's contributions on final corpus.
